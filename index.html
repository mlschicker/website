About Me

I'm a mathematics graduate student specializing in algorithms, discrete mathematics, and optimization. Currently pursuing a Master's degree at Technische Universität Berlin, I focus on mathematical modeling, algorithm design, and computational problem-solving. I'm nearing completion of my Master's program and will be writing my thesis on HUBO (Higher-Order Unconstrained Binary Optimization) under the supervision of Prof. Dr. Thorsten Koch at the Zuse Institute Berlin.

My research interests lie at the intersection of theoretical mathematics and practical applications, particularly in optimization, scheduling algorithms, and combinatorial problems. I contribute to open-source projects and collaborate with research institutions to advance computational mathematics. I've been offered a PhD position at the Zuse Institute Berlin, where I look forward to continuing my research in combinatorial optimization and algorithmic development.

Publications & Research

Quantum Optimization Benchmarking Library – The Intractable Decathlon

Thorsten Koch, David E. Bernal Neira, Ying Chen, Giorgio Cortiana, Daniel J. Egger, Raoul Heese, Narendra N. Hegade, Alejandro Gomez Cadavid, Rhea Huang, Toshinari Itoko, Thomas Kleinert, Pedro Maciel Xavier, Naeimeh Mohseni, Jhon A. Montanez-Barrera, Koji Nakano, Giacomo Nannicini, Corey O'Meara, Justin Pauckert, Manuel Proissl, Anurag Ramesh, Maximilian Schicker, Noriaki Shimada, Mitsuharu Takeori, Victor Valls, David Van Bulck, Stefan Woerner, and Christa Zoufal

arXiv preprint, 2025

A comprehensive benchmarking library for optimization algorithms, creating a "decathlon" for optimization methods with realistic benchmarks on particularly hard combinatorial problems. The project involves systematic analysis and comparison of modern optimization methods across a broad collection of challenging problems, in collaboration with IBM and international research partners.

Preprint on arXiv GitHub Repository

Bachelor Thesis: Competitive Algorithms for Non-Clairvoyant Scheduling with Lossy Preemption

Maximilian Schicker

Technische Universität Berlin, 2023

Analysis and development of competitive algorithms for scheduling problems where job durations are unknown and preemption incurs cost. Thesis completed under the supervision of Prof. Dr. Max Klimm. Achieved grade 1.0.
